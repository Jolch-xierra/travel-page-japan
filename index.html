Recommended Quick Guides

How do I easily get from Fukuoka Airport to downtown Hakata?

Fukuoka Airport is famously one of the most convenient in the world! Just take the Kuko Subway Line directly from the Domestic Terminal. It is only 2 stops (5 minutes) to Hakata Station, and 5 stops (11 minutes) to Tenjin. If arriving internationally, hop on the free green shuttle bus to the domestic terminal first.

What is the Hayakaken card and do I really need it?

The Hayakaken is Fukuoka's local IC transit card. Yes, it is highly recommended! You can load cash onto it and tap instantly on subways, buses, and JR lines. It also works in convenience stores, vending machines, and most Yatai food stalls. Suica/Pasmo cards from Tokyo are also fully accepted!

What are the unspoken rules of dining at Yatai (street food stalls)?

1. Order at least one drink (like a cold melon soda or green tea) and one food dish per person.
2. Keep your visit efficient so other waiting people can have a turn.
3. Cash is king (keep ¥1,000 bills and ¥500 coins ready).
4. Ask 'Shashin ii desu ka?' before taking close-up photos of chefs.

Where is the best place to buy tax-free anime goods and souvenirs?

Head to Canal City Hakata for official character shops (Ghibli, Jump Shop, Ultraman). For retro games, figures, and capsule toys, check out the massive 'Mandarake' and 'Animate' in Tenjin. For great souvenir sweets, Hakata Station's 'Ming' department store offers beautiful packages with duty-free counters!
